Peer-to-peer package service & online bidding. Companies like Airbnb and Uber show us that much better efficiency can be achieved in peer-to-peer services. And what better problem to solve than global logistics? Massive container ships & shipping centers result in extremely suboptimal path where packages travel back and forth. Online shoppers often have to wait massive time for shipments this is especiually bad with chinese sites like aliexpress. The argument is that a random person that just happens to have the same work trip as the package track will be significantly more efficient, cheaper, and faster than a package moving between multiple massive container ships and holding centers back and forth. Furthermore, people should be allowed to offer a bidding service. Let’s say Josh has a truck that can connect with users that pool together and bid for shipping. Let’s say people have long shipping times and pool together a better, cheaper, and faster option in a bidding platform. Basically, a crowd-sourced shipping option for every e-commerce website. This could be a huge help to solve excess capacity. Let’s say you have a truck going from A to B; you can offer the extra space for random users online, thus radically improving the global efficiency of society.
